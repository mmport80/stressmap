Understand Analyse Resource Portfolio stress testing

Reverse Engineering Armageddon

The goal of stress testing a portfolio is to ensure it is robust enough to survive future extreme events.

Model Securities

In order to create disaster 'on paper' we using mathematical simulations. E.g. by discounting bond cash flows; applying the Black-Scholes-Merton model for equity options and so on

List Inputs

Each model input is a seperate dimension of our problem

Identify Limits

Historical extreme movements of market inputs are a good starting point; which should be covered at a minimum

The limits should be less than truely apocolyptic however. E.g. if the S&P 500 index drops by more than 90% food will be more of a worry than your portfolio

The Bottom Line

How big a loss will threaten the portfolio's survival? Either through regulatory or contractual capital requirements

Putting it all Together

Where's the danger? Which scenarios cause threshold breaching losses?

Simplify

Identify dimensions where catastrophic scenarios are evenly distributed and of peripheral importance; remove them

Fail Safety Principal

Add hedge positions to the portfolio and ensure long term survival
